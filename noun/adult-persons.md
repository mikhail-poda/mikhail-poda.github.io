# Topic: Adult Personss

| **Hebrew**          | **Transcription**     | **Meaning**                                  | **Example**                                                                                                      |
| ------------------- | --------------------- | -------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **גֶּבֶר / אִשָּׁה**       | *géver / ishá*        | man / woman                                  | **הוא גבר מבוגר, והיא אישה חכמה מאוד.** – He is a grown man, and she is a very wise woman.                       |
| **אִישׁ / אִשָּׁה**       | *ísh / ishá*          | man / woman; in some contexts husband / wife | **ראיתי איש ואישה מחכים בחוץ.** – I saw a man and a woman waiting outside.                                       |
| **אָדוֹן / גְּבֶרֶת**     | *adón / gveret*       | gentleman / lady; sir / madam                | **שלום, אדוני. תודה, גברתי.** – Hello, sir. Thank you, madam.                                                    |
| **גְּבִירוֹתַי וְרַבּוֹתַי**  | *gvirótai ve-rabotai* | idiomatic "ladies and gentlemen"             | **גבירותיי ורבותיי, ברוכים הבאים לערב הזה.** – Ladies and gentlemen, welcome to this evening.                    |
| **בַּעַל / אִשָּׁה**       | *baʿál / ishá*        | husband / wife                               | **זה בעלי, וזו אשתי.** – This is my husband, and this is my wife.                                                |
| **בֶּן־זוּג / בַּת־זוּג** | *ben-zúg / bat-zúg*   | partner / spouse                             | **היא הגיעה עם בן הזוג שלה.** – She arrived with her partner.                                                    |
| **מְבוּגָר / מְבוּגֶרֶת**  | *mevugár / mevugéret* | adult; grown-up                              | **הסרט מיועד למבוגרים בלבד.** – The film is intended for adults only.                                            |

Notes:

* **גֶּבֶר** is the clearest everyday word for an **adult man**.
* **אִישׁ** can mean **man**, but in some contexts also simply **person**, and in older or formal language it can overlap with **husband**.
* **אָדוֹן / גְּבֶרֶת** are often closer to **sir / madam** than to everyday **gentleman / lady**.
* **רבותיי** does not come from a normal everyday noun meaning “gentlemen” in the same direct way English does. It is part of this formal expression.
