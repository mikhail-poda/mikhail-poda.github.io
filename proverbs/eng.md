# Hebrew Proverbs translated to English

## Part 1

| Original Hebrew proverb                    | Exact translation                                                                                               | Corresponding English proverb                                            | Explanation, if needed                                                                                       |
| ------------------------------------------ | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| אין הנחתום מעיד על עיסתו                   | The baker does not testify about his own dough.                                                                 | Self-praise is no recommendation.                                        |                          |
| אין עשן בלי אש                             | There is no smoke without fire.                                                                                 | Where there’s smoke, there’s fire.                                       |                                                                                                             |
| אל תסתכל בקנקן, אלא במה שיש בו             | Do not look at the jar, but at what is inside it.                                                               | Don’t judge a book by its cover.                                         |                                                                                                             |
| אל תקנה חתול בשק                           | Do not buy a cat in a sack.                                                                                     | Don’t buy a pig in a poke.                                               |                                    |
| הוי זנב לאריות ואל תהי ראש לשועלים         | Be a tail among lions, and do not be a head among foxes.                                                        | Better to be a small fish in a big pond than a big fish in a small pond. |              |
| טובה ציפור אחת ביד משתיים על העץ           | One bird in the hand is better than two in the tree.                                                            | A bird in the hand is worth two in the bush.                             |                                                                                                            |
| כי בתחבולות תעשה לך מלחמה                  | For by strategic planning you shall wage war.                                                                   | The battle is won before it is fought.                                   | Here, **תחבולות** means strategies, plans or skilful counsel, not merely dishonest tricks.                   |
| כל המרחם על אכזרים, סופו להתאכזר על רחמנים | Whoever shows mercy to the cruel will eventually be cruel to the merciful.                                      | Mercy to the guilty is cruelty to the innocent.                          | Misplaced mercy toward cruel people may allow them to continue harming innocent people.                      |
| הכה בברזל בעודו חם                         | Strike the iron while it is hot.                                                                                | Strike while the iron is hot.                                            |                                                                                                            |
| מה ששנוא עליך אל תעשה לחברך                | What is hateful to you, do not do to your fellow.                                                               | Do unto others as you would have them do unto you.                       |                                                         |
| מרוב עצים לא רואים את היער                 | Because of the many trees, one cannot see the forest.                                                           | You can’t see the forest for the trees.                                  |                                                                                                            |
| נאה דורש, נאה מקיים                        | One who expounds well also fulfils well.                                                                        | Practice what you preach.                                                | The person who teaches or demands something should also act accordingly.                                     |
| סנונית אחת אינה מבשרת את בוא האביב         | One swallow does not herald the arrival of spring.                                                              | One swallow does not make a summer.                                      | A single occurrence is not enough to establish a general pattern.                                            |
| על דאטפת אטפוך, וסוף מטיפייך יטופון        | Because you drowned others, they drowned you; and in the end, those who drowned you will themselves be drowned. | What goes around comes around.                                           | This is an Aramaic saying about retribution: wrongdoing ultimately brings punishment upon the wrongdoer.     |
| צוחק מי שצוחק אחרון                        | He who laughs last, laughs.                                                                                     | He who laughs last laughs best.                                          |                                            |
| צרות באות בצרורות                          | Troubles come in bundles.                                                                                       | Misfortunes never come singly.                                           | Also comparable to “When it rains, it pours.”                                                                |
| רחוק מהעין, רחוק מהלב                      | Far from the eye, far from the heart.                                                                           | Out of sight, out of mind.                                               |                                                                                                             |
| תפסת מרובה – לא תפסת                       | If you grasped too much, you grasped nothing.                                                                   | If you chase two rabbits, you will catch neither.                        |                        |
| לא כל יום פורים                            | Not every day is Purim.                                                                                         | Every day can’t be a holiday.                                            |     |
| כל ישראל ערבים זה בזה                      | All Israel are guarantors for one another.                                                                      | All for one and one for all.                                             | **ערבים** implies mutual responsibility and accountability, not merely general friendliness or solidarity.   |
| ואהבת לרעך כמוך                            | You shall love your neighbour as yourself.                                                                      | Love thy neighbour as thyself.                                           | This is a biblical commandment rather than an ordinary folk proverb.                                         |
| איזהו עשיר? השמח בחלקו                     | Who is rich? The person who rejoices in their portion.                                                          | Contentment is better than riches.                                       | True wealth consists in appreciating what one has.                                                           |
| איזהו חכם? הלומד מכל אדם                   | Who is wise? The person who learns from every human being.                                                      | A wise person learns from everyone.                                      |                                                                                                            |
| איזהו גיבור? הכובש את יצרו                 | Who is mighty? The person who conquers their own inclination.                                                   | The greatest victory is victory over oneself.                            | **יצרו** refers to one’s impulses, urges or inclinations; true strength is self-control.                     |
| אם אין אני לי, מי לי?                      | If I am not for myself, who will be for me?                                                                     | You have to stand up for yourself.                                       | The saying stresses personal responsibility and the need to take action on one’s own behalf.                 |
| ואם לא עכשיו, אימתי?                       | And if not now, when?                                                                                           | There’s no time like the present.                                        |                                                                                                            |
| אמור מעט ועשה הרבה                         | Say little and do much.                                                                                         | Actions speak louder than words.                                         |                                                                                                           |

## Part 2

| Original Hebrew proverb                                                                      | Exact translation                                                                                                                                          | Corresponding English proverb                                        | Explanation, if needed                                                                                                                                             |

| אכול ושתו כי מחר נמות                                                                        | Eat and drink, for tomorrow we shall die.                                                                                                                  | Eat, drink and be merry, for tomorrow we die.                        | Enjoy the present because life is short and uncertain.                                                                                                             |
| אם אין אני לי, מי לי? וכשאני לעצמי, מה אני? ואם לא עכשיו, אימתי?                             | If I am not for myself, who is for me? And when I am only for myself, what am I? And if not now, when?                                                     | There’s no time like the present.                                    |                                                        |
| מצא מין את מינו                                                                              | One kind found its own kind.                                                                                                                               | Birds of a feather flock together.                                   |                                                                                               |
| ואהבת לרעך כמוך                                                                              | You shall love your neighbour as yourself.                                                                                                                 | Love thy neighbour as thyself.                                       | This is a biblical commandment.                                                                                               |
| כל אהבה שהיא תלויה בדבר, בטל דבר – בטלה אהבה; ושאינה תלויה בדבר, אינה בטלה לעולם             | Any love that depends on something: when that thing ceases, the love ceases; but love that does not depend on something never ceases.                      | True love never dies.                                                | Love based only on some benefit or circumstance ends when its cause disappears.                                                                                    |
| כל הפוסל במומו פוסל                                                                          | Whoever declares another unfit does so through their own defect.                                                                                           | The pot calling the kettle black.                                    | People’s criticism of others often reveals their own faults.                                                                                                       |
| עבר יומו – בטל קרבנו                                                                         | Its day has passed; its sacrifice is invalid.                                                                                                              | You missed the boat.                                                 | An opportunity or deadline has passed, and a late attempt can no longer replace what was required at the proper time.                                              |
| לכל זמן, ועת לכל חפץ תחת השמים                                                               | For everything there is a season, and a time for every purpose under heaven.                                                                               | Everything in its own time.                                          |                                                                                                                                                                   |
| תפסת מרובה – לא תפסת                                                                         | If you grasped too much, you grasped nothing.                                                                                                              | Don’t bite off more than you can chew.                               | Attempting too much at once may leave you with nothing.                                                                                                            |
| חזית איש מהיר במלאכתו? לפני מלכים יתייצב                                                     | Have you seen a person skilled in their work? They shall stand before kings.                                                                               | A good workman is known by his work.                                 | Exceptional skill brings recognition and access to important people.                                                                                               |
| לעולם יאכל אדם פחות מן הראוי לו לפי ממונו, וילבש כראוי לו, ויכבד אשתו ובניו יותר מן הראוי לו | A person should always eat below what their means allow, dress according to their means, and honour their wife and children beyond what their means allow. | Live within your means.                                              |                          |
| על ראש הגנב בוער הכובע                                                                       | The hat is burning on the thief’s head.                                                                                                                    | A guilty conscience needs no accuser.                                | A guilty person’s nervous or defensive behaviour often exposes them.                                                                                               |
| אין דבר העומד בפני הרצון                                                                     | Nothing can stand before the will.                                                                                                                         | Where there’s a will, there’s a way.                                 |                                                                                                                                                                   |
| קנה חכמה, מה טוב מחרוץ; וקנות בינה נבחר מכסף                                                 | Acquiring wisdom is so much better than gold; acquiring understanding is preferable to silver.                                                             | Wisdom is better than riches.                                        |                                                                                                                                                                   |
| איזהו חכם? הלומד מכל אדם                                                                     | Who is wise? The person who learns from every human being.                                                                                                 | A wise person learns from everyone.                                  |                                                                                                                                                                   |
| כי ברוב חכמה רב כעס, ויוסיף דעת יוסיף מכאוב                                                  | For with much wisdom comes much vexation, and one who increases knowledge increases pain.                                                                  | Ignorance is bliss.                                                  | Greater understanding can also bring greater awareness of suffering and problems.                                                                                  |
| צרת רבים חצי נחמה                                                                            | The trouble of many is half a consolation.                                                                                                                 | A trouble shared is a trouble halved.                                |                                                                                           |
| אילני סרק קולם הולך                                                                          | The sound of barren trees carries far.                                                                                                                     | Empty vessels make the most noise.                                   | People with little knowledge or substance are often the loudest and most boastful.                                                                                 |
| אין חכם כבעל ניסיון                                                                          | There is no one as wise as a person with experience.                                                                                                       | Experience is the best teacher.                                      |                                                                                                                                                                   |
| לא הביישן למד ולא הקפדן מלמד                                                                 | The bashful person does not learn, and the overly strict person does not teach.                                                                            | There are no foolish questions.                                      |                                                      |
| דברי חכמים בנחת נשמעים                                                                       | The words of the wise are heard in quietness.                                                                                                              | You catch more flies with honey than with vinegar.                   | Calm and gentle speech is often more persuasive than shouting or aggression.                                                                                       |
| סייג לחכמה שתיקה                                                                             | Silence is a fence around wisdom.                                                                                                                          | Speech is silver, silence is golden.                                 |                                                 |
| בור ששתית ממנו אל תזרוק בו אבן                                                               | Do not throw a stone into a well from which you have drunk.                                                                                                | Don’t bite the hand that feeds you.                                  |                                                                                       |
| אל יתהלל חוגר כמפתח                                                                          | Let not one who puts on their armour boast like one who takes it off.                                                                                      | Don’t count your chickens before they hatch.                         | Do not boast about victory before the challenge or struggle is over.                                                                                               |
| חושך שבטו שונא בנו                                                                           | One who withholds the rod hates his son.                                                                                                                   | Spare the rod and spoil the child.                                   | Traditionally, this means that a parent who refuses to discipline a child does the child a disservice; it need not be understood as endorsing physical punishment. |
| שלח לחמך על פני המים, כי ברוב הימים תמצאנו                                                   | Cast your bread upon the waters, for after many days you shall find it.                                                                                    | You reap what you sow.                                               | Generosity or a worthwhile investment may bring a return after a long time.                                                                                        |
| טובים השניים מן האחד                                                                         | The two are better than the one.                                                                                                                           | Two heads are better than one.                                       | Cooperation allows people to achieve more than either could achieve alone.                                                                                         |
